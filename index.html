About me:

I completed my undergrad in Civil Engineering, but my journey took a strange turn when I received an offer for an entry-level data analyst role from Accenture in 2018. I had very little coding and analytics experience prior to this point, but I learned programming skills such as SQL and Python on the job. Between 2018 and 2022, I worked for four years in the data consulting industry and had the privilege of working with reputable companies like Infosys and Deloitte after leaving Accenture.

The grueling lockdown of the COVID-19 pandemic shook me to the core and threw me into an existential crisis, causing me to question the direction of my career. After the lockdown ended, I made a conscious decision to leave my job at Deloitte and move to the United States to pursue a Master's degree in data science. I saw this as an opportunity to build a strong foundation before transitioning my career from consulting to pure data science.

I successfully completed one year in the United States now. Despite battling social anxiety and homesickness, I excelled academically with a perfect 4.0 CGPA and secured a data science summer internship with ISN Software Corporation in Dallas. In addition to my beautiful and chaotic journey, I possess a deep passion for writing. I combine this passion with the data science skills I am acquiring to create content on Medium, LinkedIn, and my blog (dumbdatum.com).
